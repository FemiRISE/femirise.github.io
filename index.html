Mission & Vision

Our six pillars

01
Feminist Literacy

Making feminism accessible. Including education on the core values of the feminist movement, our history, key figures, and feminist literature.

02
Documentation and Archival

Documenting and sharing the latest global developments in feminism through essays, debates, and our podcast.

03
Thoughtful Discourse

Fostering thoughtful, nuanced discussions and debates about feminism.

04
Safe Space

A secure, supportive platform where women share their experiences, reclaim their narratives, and find community.

05
Tech & Innovation

Pioneering programs, initiatives and technologies designed to enhance the safety, wellbeing and empowerment of women.

∞
Scalable Impact

Through chapters, mentorship and a transparent committee system, we build an organisation that grows with intention and integrity.

Structure

Three paths to impact

I
General Member

Low-commitment participation. Join events, connect with local chapters, and fuel your feminist journey.

II
Chapter Leader

Establish and lead a chapter in your region. Organise monthly events, recruit your team, and document your impact.

III
Committee Member

The backbone of FemiRISE. Apply every 6 months across Events, PR, Secretariat, and Chapter Mentoring.

Membership

How we work together

FemiRISE operates through three interconnected membership tiers, each designed for a different level of commitment and impact.

I
Tier One
General Members
Low Commitment
  • Participate in events, initiatives and programs hosted by the organisation
  • Join existing chapters in their region, state or country
  • Apply to be a Chapter Leader if no chapter exists in your region yet
Getting Started
1
Fill in the Recruitment Form

Complete our general member application form — it takes just a few minutes.

2
Receive Email Updates

We'll email you with updates on upcoming events, programs and initiatives relevant to you.

II
Tier Two
Chapter Leaders
High Impact
  • Establish and officially register a chapter in your region, state or country
  • Recruit and lead a team to organise events — minimum once per month (exceptions granted with management notification)
  • Survey and document the impact of each event — this is mandatory and integral to our mission
  • A Chapter Mentor can be assigned upon request to guide you through the process
Getting Started
1
Fill in the Recruitment Form

Submit your Chapter Leader application with your region and initial vision.

2
Schedule a Committee Meeting

Book a session via Calendly to confirm your position. Come prepared with questions and a rough plan.

3
Establish Chapter Channels

Set up official communication channels (Instagram, Discord, WhatsApp etc.) aligned with local needs and organisational guidelines.

4
Begin Recruiting & Your First Event

Start building your local team and plan your inaugural event. You've got this — and we're here to support you every step of the way.

III
Tier Three
Committee Members
Applications Every 6 Months
  • Applications open exclusively to the pool of active Chapter Leaders, every 6 months
  • Departments: Events & Curricula, PR & Marketing, Secretariat, and Chapter Mentors
  • Work directly with the co-founders and core team as the backbone of FemiRISE
  • Weekly meetings required; absences must be notified to management in advance
★ Chapter Mentors
Guiding the next generation of leaders

Chapter Mentors are a specialised group within the Committee — highly experienced individuals in the management and logistics of running chapters. They serve as trusted guides for Chapter Leaders navigating the complexities of building a local feminist community. A mentor can be requested by any Chapter Leader who needs additional support.

Policies
📅 Event Frequency

Chapter Leaders are expected to organise events at least once per month. Exceptions may be granted upon notifying management with sufficient notice.

📊 Impact Surveys

After every event, chapter leaders must conduct impact surveys and documentation. This data shapes our strategy and demonstrates our collective footprint.

📆 Weekly Meetings

Committee members attend weekly meetings. If absent, notify management beforehand — communication is the foundation of our collective.

⚖️ Applications

Committee positions open every 6 months to active Chapter Leaders. This keeps leadership fresh, inclusive and grounded in chapter experience.
